Smart Waste Practices for Citizens

Reporting is great, but reducing waste starts with us!

Segregation at Source

Keep Wet Waste (food scraps) and Dry Waste (paper, plastic, metal) in separate bins. This increases recycling efficiency by 70%.

Home Composting

Turn your kitchen peels into "Black Gold" for your plants. It reduces the load on city landfills and prevents bad odors.

The 5 R’s

Refuse single-use plastics, Reduce what you buy, Reuse containers, Repurpose old items, and Recycle as a last resort.

E-Waste Disposal

Never throw batteries or old electronics in the regular trash. Take them to designated E-waste collection centers to prevent soil poisoning.
